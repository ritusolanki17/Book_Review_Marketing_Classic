Book Review · Marketing Classic

Positioning:
The Battle for Your Mind

Al Ries & Jack Trout

📅 First published 1981 📖 22 Chapters covered ⭐ Marketing bible for decades

Before Instagram, before personal branding, before every hotel claimed to be "a luxury experience" — Ries and Trout said something radical: the real battle isn't in the marketplace. It's in the mind. This book, written in 1981, still reads like a mirror held up to every brand decision you make today.

5 Timeless Takeaways

What the book leaves you with

01

Be first or find a gap. The mind doesn't reward the best — it rewards the first it remembers.

02

Your brand lives in someone else's head. All your marketing is a conversation with perception, not reality.

03

Simplicity wins. One word. One idea. One ownable space in the market.

04

Line extension is the enemy. Spreading thin dilutes the very thing that made you strong.

05

The name is the strategy. Choose it like your brand depends on it — because it does.
